Some common types of mental health disorders

Anxiety disorders

a group of mental health conditions characterized by excessive and persistent feelings of anxiety, worry, fear, and apprehension

Mood disorders

a group of mental health conditions characterized by disturbances in a person's emotional state or mood

Eating disorders

a group of mental health conditions characterized by disordered eating patterns and a preoccupation with food, weight, and body image

Psychotic disorders

a group of mental health conditions characterized by a disconnection from reality, which can manifest as delusions, hallucinations, disorganized thinking, and abnormal behaviors

Stories

Story of John Ridlehoover

Ridlehoover has been a professional writer for 10 years and has been writing for 20 years. He’s half Filipino and grew up in a small Southern town. He was the only Asian kid in every school he attended before high school. He said that “I didn’t notice until another kid pointed it out to me. Then it was all I could see.”

Story of Jacob Zheng

Many high school students, especially Asian American students, are feeling intense pressure to gain admission to a prestigious college. Since Asian culture is heavily tied to success in academics, his Asian American friends and him have been anxious over seeing the lowered admission rates for top colleges. To make matters worse, Asian American students are often told by their parents that they are failures for not getting into Harvard, Stanford, UCLA — the list goes on.
